If you've ever tried to buy surplus equipment, vehicles, or electronics from government auctions, you know the pain: there are at least five different websites you need to check, each with its own account, search system, and alert setup.

**GovGrab fixes that.** We aggregate listings from GSA Auctions, PublicSurplus, Municibid, and more into a single search engine with unified alerts.

## The Problem

Government agencies at every level — federal, state, county, and municipal — sell surplus property through online auctions. The deals are real: police cruisers for $3,000, commercial laptops for $50, excavators for pennies on the dollar.

But the market is fragmented across platforms that don't talk to each other:

- **GSA Auctions** handles federal surplus (military, FEMA, DOL)
- **PublicSurplus** covers state and county agencies
- **Municibid** serves municipal governments and school districts
- **GovDeals** is the largest state/local platform
- **GovPlanet** specializes in heavy and military equipment

Each platform has its own registration, its own search, and its own alert system. If you want to find a Ford F-250 within 200 miles, you need to search all five sites separately. Every day.

## How GovGrab Works

GovGrab pulls listings from multiple platforms into one unified search:

1. **Search once, see everything.** Type "excavator" and see results from GSA, PublicSurplus, and Municibid in one page.

2. **Filter by what matters.** State, category, price range, ending soonest — all filters work across all platforms simultaneously.

3. **Know the true cost.** Different platforms charge different buyer's premiums. GSA charges nothing. Municibid charges 9%. GovGrab shows you the true cost including all fees.

4. **Save searches and get alerts.** Set up a saved search for "laptop" in your state. When new listings appear, you'll know.

## Who Uses GovGrab

- **Resellers and flippers** who source inventory from surplus auctions
- **Small businesses** buying equipment at 30-70% below retail
- **Independent dealers** looking for fleet vehicles
- **Contractors** finding tools, trucks, and heavy equipment
- **Anyone** who wants government surplus deals without the hassle
